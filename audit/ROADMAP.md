# ROADMAP: 30/60/90 to NFL launch

Written 2026-07-11, closing the pre-launch audit. Sequenced by dependency and cost-to-change, not excitement. NOW is due about August 10, NEXT about September 10 (launch window), LATER about October 10 (mid-season). Sources: audit sections 10 through 60, TODO.md, OPERATIONS.md.

## 1. The strategy in five sentences

The niche is the burned tailer, a recreational parlay bettor who has paid for picks before and got burned, so the sale is the same money for picks that come with receipts. The flagship product is machine-built graded parlays, assembled from verified positive-edge legs, graded before the bet and settled publicly after, a category nobody in the competitive set currently occupies. The proof asset is The House Ledger, a named, public, append-only settlement record that includes every loser and every correct Trap call, built to FTC substantiation standard. The channel is organic receipts culture on Instagram plus Reddit and betting Twitter, run by an automated ledger-to-graphic-to-post pipeline, with paid ads treated as a phase-two authorization project rather than a launch lever. The price is $19.99 per month with a 7-day no-card trial, sitting on the verified entry-tier anchor, justified by graded answers rather than by being cheap, with an annual plan and a second tier deferred until the ledger has a public season behind it.

## 2. NOW (by ~Aug 10): launch-blocking work in priority order

1. **ESPN data migration.** Move scores, standings, and stats off unofficial ESPN endpoints to a licensed source such as The Odds API scores or SportsDataIO. It is the one legal blocker before charging money (Disney ToU breach inside a paid product) and the standings sync feeds every downstream analysis, so it must land and soak before NFL data flows. Effort: 3 to 5 days including a parallel-run comparison week.
2. **Lock-time data integrity fix.** Unify to one lock payload with real odds and the correct model label, replacing the hardcoded -110 odds and the stamped model name on the digest path. The ledger is the entire pitch and it is currently recording fiction on one of two paths, and everything below builds on ledger credibility. Effort: 1 to 2 days.
3. **Billing with click-to-cancel compliance.** Stripe checkout and customer portal, trial state in the client, price and renewal terms adjacent to the pay button, separate unchecked auto-renewal consent, confirmation email, one-click cancellation, Stripe Tax on, and the account profile explicitly described as informational sports analysis with no wagering. California's auto-renewal law is the national floor and it is cheap to build right once and expensive to retrofit, and the page currently promises a trial no mechanism enforces. Also pre-qualify one high-risk backup processor now, before any freeze. Effort: 4 to 6 days plus half a day on the backup processor.
4. **Machine-built graded parlays MVP.** Combos from verified positive-edge legs, signed per-leg edges, a combined graded score, correlation handled at least by exclusion rules, every settled parlay written to the ledger. This is the flagship the whole positioning rests on, the section 20 tripwires say the differentiation window is open now and may not stay open, and it must be settling cleanly before Week 1. Get the one-hour gaming-attorney read before it ships and label outputs as illustrations of model output. Effort: 5 to 8 days plus the attorney hour.
5. **The House Ledger productization.** A named public ledger page at its own URL, append-only presentation, headline number scoped exactly (tier, period, grading method), timestamps, ROI and units alongside win rate, Trap restyled so a correct Trap reads as a win, and a dated snapshot process for every stat that ships in content. It is the front door of all marketing and the FTC substantiation file at the same time, and the Instagram pipeline consumes it. Effort: 3 to 4 days.
6. **Product-UX high-priority fixes.** Google OAuth first-run routed to the digest, email-confirmation state, real HTML metadata and favicon with prerendered landing, framing copy for the losing public tiers, admin auth moved server-side, ticker reordered so the first impression is not six red Traps, invented competitor numbers removed, no-card-required caption under the hero CTA, quiet-day digest fallback. These are the things a paying week-one customer hits, and the shared-link metadata must exist before the Instagram account starts driving clicks. Effort: 4 to 6 days total.
7. **Trademark filing and legal hygiene.** One-hour attorney clearance review, then intent-to-use filing for the full name "Cray Cray for Parlays" in Class 41 with The House Ledger mark alongside, plus the single-member LLC, business bank account, attorney-adapted ToS with the nine-clause checklist, and the trade-secret checklist (NDA templates with DTSA notice, dated internal description of the secret). Filing sets priority before the brand gets public exposure, and the LLC and ToS must exist before the first dollar of revenue. Effort: 2 to 3 founder days spread across attorney turnaround, roughly $1,500 to $3,000 cash.
8. **Instagram account start plus automation pipeline.** Professional account with the compliant bio and standing disclaimer, developer app, Satori card templates, R2 hosting, Graph API publish flow on the existing Railway cron, ManyChat comment-to-DM funnel, and the 9-post seed grid. The 30-day calendar needs day 1 about two weeks before Week 1, so the pipeline must be running by mid-August, and the account needs a body of clean content before any ads authorization attempt. Effort: 5 to 7 days, roughly $30 to $50 per month run cost.

Deliberately not in NOW: the routing extraction and generator rebuild (structural, lands after launch), the rename question (closed, EVOLVE decision stands), and the silent-sports fixes (NFL launch does not depend on tennis, UFC, or soccer).

## 3. NEXT (by ~Sept 10, launch)

1. **NFL seasonal flip.** Update seasonal_context in ai_instructions in late August so NFL pick generation turns on. It is a small manual change with a hard external deadline, and NFL edges start at the 0.75 global calibration multiplier until 80 settled picks per market, so early volume matters. Effort: hours, plus daily observation the first week.
2. **Launch checklist pass.** End-to-end dry run of the full funnel: trial signup on both auth paths, digest first screen, parlay flagship, checkout, cancellation, ledger accuracy, deep-link degradation in non-legal states, disclaimers, and the marketing-copy scrub against the substantiation standard. Everything shipped in NOW gets verified as a system, because a niche defined by having been burned will not forgive a broken trial promise in week one. Effort: 2 to 3 days including the fixed-fee attorney copy review.
3. **Week-one settlement verification.** Confirm settlement grades NFL correctly, watch the first Monday refresh_edge_calibration runs, and manually audit the first settled parlays against the ledger. The first public NFL receipts are the trust moment, and a wrong grade published in week one costs more than any feature. Effort: 2 to 4 founder hours per day during Week 1.
4. **Content engine at full cadence.** Automated pick card each morning and graded receipt each night, Trap of the day 3 to 5 times per week, 2 to 3 human founder posts weekly, comment-keyword funnel live on every pick post, first weekly ledger recap after Week 1. Week 1 is proof week and the receipts pillar only works if losses post on time from day one. Effort: automated anchors run themselves, 4 to 6 founder hours per week on human content.
5. **Paid-ads authorization attempt.** File the Meta Business Suite authorization request under the otherwise-established-as-lawful clause, documenting no bets held, no funds moved, no operator links. The account now has a clean content body behind it, the review is slow and manual so it should start early, and if it fails organic remains the plan with no workaround creative. Effort: half a day to file, then a waiting game.

## 4. LATER (by ~Oct 10)

1. **Annual plan.** Ship $119.99 to $149.99 annual once four to five weeks of public NFL ledger exist to sell against. It locks in the season-long buyer at the moment mid-season churn risk starts. Effort: 1 day.
2. **Second tier, gated on the public season.** Hold the $39.99 to $49.99 tier until a full public season is on the ledger, per the section 10 decision. Scope it now on paper only, so October is a decision checkpoint rather than a build. Effort: hours of planning.
3. **CLV publicization.** Turn the internal v_pick_clv reporting into a public proof point once several weeks of closing-line data accumulate. Beating the close is the sharpest credibility stat in the category and nobody at the consumer tier publishes it. Effort: 2 to 3 days.
4. **Scale decisions.** With four weeks of real data, decide the paid-ads budget if authorized, whether the free ledger-visible teaser tier is needed against OddsJam's free funnel, whether to start the generator rebuild and routing extraction, and whether the silently failing sports get a data path or a formal drop from coverage claims. These choices are cheap to make correctly in October and expensive to guess in August. Effort: a planning day, informed by the weekly readouts.

## 5. Open risks carried forward

- **Attach rate unknown.** The SOM math rests on an illustrative 1 to 3 percent paid attach rate with no verified source, tagged [ASSUMPTION] in section 10. Plan against thousands of subscribers, not tens of thousands, and treat the first 90 days of trial-to-paid conversion as the real number.
- **OddsJam fast-follow.** If OddsJam attaches graded results to its free picks funnel, or Pikkit wires real edge math into its parlay generator, the "only" in the positioning statement breaks. Check both tripwires monthly, and the response is speed on the parlay flagship, not a pivot.
- **Sweep still maturing.** The data-integrity agent sweep is weeks old, its intel does not yet move the math, and a Railway redeploy kills an in-flight run. Watch agent_error rows weekly and do not market its outputs until calibration proves a signal.
- **[ASSUMPTION]-tagged figures.** The market-size and SOM numbers from section 10, the 5 to 15 percent free-to-paid benchmark from section 50, and competitor prices captured 2026-07-11 all decay. Recheck pricing before any pricing change ships.
- **Processor and platform risk.** Stripe can freeze a gambling-adjacent account preemptively and Meta classifiers can flag organic content. The backup processor and the compliance guardrails are the mitigations, already scheduled above.
