# Competitive Landscape

Cray Cray for Parlays business audit, section 20. Based on per-competitor findings verified against live sites in July 2026. Competitors covered are Action Network, OddsJam, BetQL, Outlier, Pikkit, and Rithmm.

Two patterns matter. First, the language is converging on ours. Action Network now markets "edges," OddsJam markets "math," and Rithmm claims "proven model edges." The words are getting crowded even though the substance is not. Second, four of six competitors now touch our $19.99 price point in some form, so price is no longer a standalone advantage.

## 2. Track-record transparency

This is the clearest structural gap in the market. We publish a settlement ledger covering every pick, winners and losers, with a 63.5% top-tier win rate. No competitor publishes anything comparable.

| Company | What they publish | Auditable? | The gap versus our ledger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Action Network | Nothing for their own experts. Community bet verification only, and reviewers report verified bets can be deleted. | No | They verify their users, not themselves. No graded house record exists. |
| OddsJam | Nothing. An income promise of $500 to $1,000 weekly with zero receipts. Pikkit badges on cherry-picked testimonials. | No | The boldest claim in the market with the least backing. Trustpilot fell to 2.9. |
| BetQL | Cherry-picked hot-streak windows (3 to 30 days, sliced by team and market). Some stat cards render broken. | No | Survivorship framing. Only the hot slices are shown, never the full history. |
| Outlier | Nothing. One user testimonial ROI figure. Tool framing lets them avoid accountability entirely. | No | No outcomes are tracked for anything they surface. |
| Pikkit | Verified user records synced from sportsbooks, no self-reporting. The strongest transparency in the set. | Partly | Real verification, but of individual users, and the leaderboard surfaces winners. It is a survivor list, not a house record. |
| Rithmm | Nothing official. A "4M+ winning predictions" marketing claim taken on faith. User-side trackers only. | No | A rival (PropsBot) already attacks them publicly on this exact gap. |

Pikkit is the only one that can honestly say "verified, no cherry-picking," so our messaging must be precise. Theirs is a leaderboard of surviving winners. Ours is a house ledger that includes every published pick, including the losers.

## 3. Parlay features

We plan machine-built parlay combinations assembled from verified positive-edge legs, graded before the bet and settled publicly after it. Here is what the field offers today.

| Company | Builds parlays by machine? | Grades legs? | Correlation aware? | Settles and publishes results? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Action Network | No. Calculators, QuickSlip tap-to-track, slip-parsing bot. Convenience, not math. | No | No | No |
| OddsJam | Yes. Parlay Builder generates combos from +EV-only legs, in the ~$198/mo tier. | Positive-only, no signed edges | Not shown | No |
| BetQL | No. Parlay content is blog posts and a human-curated daily column. | No | No | No |
| Outlier | No. Betslip auto-fill and trending parlays to tail. Users assemble legs manually. | No | No | No |
| Pikkit | Yes, but explicitly random. Parlay Mystery Box is entertainment, not edge math. Plus one-tap parlay copying and SGP line shopping. | No | No | No |
| Rithmm | Assists, does not generate. Scans user-built parlays, flags weak legs, suggests swaps. | Flags weak legs, no signed magnitudes | Editorially, via content warnings | No |

The takeaway is that OddsJam owns "build," Rithmm owns "critique," Pikkit owns "copy," and nobody owns "grade and settle." A parlay product that shows signed per-leg edges, accounts for correlation, and publishes settled outcomes on a public ledger has no direct competitor in this set.

## 4. Top 3 threats

1. **Price convergence with superior distribution.** Action Network promos, BetQL Starter, Outlier Premium, and Rithmm annualized Core all now sit at or near $19.99. Each has distribution we cannot match, including app store scale, radio networks, SEO estates, and free trials. Our May-era price umbrella is gone, and a side-by-side shopper sees established platforms at our price.
2. **OddsJam's free daily picks funnel.** A $160M-backed brand is now emailing free "data-backed bets" to a mass audience, training our exact prospect pool to expect math-graded picks for $0. They also hold the strongest parlay-building engine in the market. If they bolt a graded results page onto that funnel, they close most of our transparency gap overnight.
3. **Fast-follow risk on graded parlays.** Pikkit's Mystery Box proves they will generate bets, and they hold sportsbook-synced outcome data plus verified-record credibility. Rithmm already scans parlay legs for weakness. OddsJam already builds +EV combos. Any of the three could wire existing machinery into an edge-informed, graded parlay product faster than we can build an audience.

## 5. Top 3 whitespace opportunities, ranked

1. **The auditable house record.** No competitor publishes a graded ledger of its own output, and two of them (OddsJam and Rithmm) make aggressive claims with no receipts while reviewers and rivals attack them for it. "They promise income, we publish receipts" is a direct-contrast campaign that every competitor is structurally unable to answer without exposing its own record.
2. **Graded, settled parlays.** The parlay category splits into builders, critics, and copiers, and none of them scores a parlay's combined edge or publishes settled parlay outcomes. Our planned product, machine-built combos from verified positive-edge legs with correlation-aware grading and public settlement, is an unoccupied category rather than a feature race.
3. **Signed negative edges and the Trap label.** Every competitor surfaces only positive opportunities. BetQL's lowest rating means "low value," not "actively bad." OddsJam's builder is positive-only by design. Nobody in the market tells a bettor what not to bet as a graded, accountable call. Closely related and worth bundling in messaging, our no-affiliate economics contrast cleanly with BetQL and Action Network, whose businesses depend on sportsbook payouts.

## Implications for the roadmap

- Ship the parlay product as the flagship, not a feature. Machine-built combos from verified positive-edge legs, with per-leg signed edges and every settled parlay added to the public ledger, is the one offering nobody in the market can currently copy without rebuilding their accountability model.
- Make the settlement ledger the front door of all marketing. Lead with "audit our 63.5%, then ask them for theirs," and name the contrast explicitly against OddsJam's income promise and BetQL's hot-streak windows.
- Launch the Trap label publicly and grade it on the ledger. Negative-edge honesty is uncontested territory and it compounds the transparency story, since publishing losers is what makes the ledger believable.
- Stop relying on price. At $19.99 we are now matched by four competitors, so the tier must be justified by graded answers and receipts, not by being cheap. Consider a free, ledger-visible teaser tier to counter OddsJam's free funnel before it trains our prospects.
- Track two tripwires quarterly. If OddsJam attaches graded results to its free picks, or Pikkit wires real edge math into its parlay generator, our differentiation window narrows and the parlay flagship needs to already be live.
