Six promoted capabilities on the homepage: Social, Line Shop, Copy, Live Track, BookSync, Analyze. Still no picks, edges, win-rate claims, or AI messaging anywhere on the homepage.

The Discover surface adds a Following Leaderboard ranked by ROI with win-loss records, a Consistent Winners section with weekly winning streaks and an ROI trend graph, and an influencer leaderboard now filterable by sport and bet type. Their blog explicitly downplays win rate as "the most misleading stat" and pushes ROI instead. Important caveat: this is verification of user and influencer records, not of a house model. Pikkit still publishes no picks of its own, so there is no product-level graded history to compare against our 63.5% ledger.

**Parlay features (this is the material change since May)**:

This is machine-built parlay generation, but it is explicitly random and entertainment-framed, with no edge math behind it. First observed on the live site in this scan; not present in the May profile.
- SGP Lineshopping remains Pro-gated: "Build same-game parlays in app and compare prices - exclusively on Pikkit" (pikkit.com/pro).

**Other changes since May 2026**:

- No funding, pivot, or pricing news in June-July 2026. Still roughly 25 employees (CB Insights, May 2026), last raise the 2022 seed, $9.4M total.

**Revised threat read**: the Trap-label and pre-bet-edge whitespace from the May profile still holds, but two hedges. First, Parlay Mystery Box shows Pikkit is now willing to *generate* bets, not just track them. It is random today, but if they wire their CLV and ROI data into it, they have the distribution and verified-record credibility to launch an edge-informed parlay builder quickly. Second, their verified-record leaderboard is a genuinely auditable trust mechanism at the user level, which blunts our "transparency" pitch unless we emphasize that ours is a *house* record covering every published pick, including the losers, not a leaderboard of survivors. Survivorship bias in "Consistent Winners" is the counterargument to have ready.

## At a Glance
- **Tagline**: "All your friends and bets in one place. Follow, copy, bet."
- **What they actually do**: Pikkit is a bet-tracking app that auto-syncs to 30+ US sportsbooks (no manual entry), then layers analytics (CLV, performance by sport/bet type), a social feed, line shopping, and promo tracking on top. It is *not* a sportsbook, does not accept wagers, and does not generate picks via a model — it grades *you*, not the game.
- **Target audience**: Multi-book recreational and "sharp-curious" bettors who already place real wagers and want to know if they're +EV. Social-feed framing widens it toward followers/copy-bettors. Not aimed at degens looking for action ideas; aimed at the post-bet audit + pre-bet line shop.
- **Founded / scale**: iOS app live since 2021 (App Store ID 1586567110). 4.9/5 App Store rating. Frequently described in 2025-26 reviews as "the most popular free sports bet tracking app." Specific funding/team data not surfaced in this scan.

## Positioning & Messaging
- **Primary value prop (homepage)**: "All your friends and bets in one place. Follow, copy, bet." Sub: "Sync your sportsbooks, share picks, shop lines, and track your performance with Pikkit." (source: https://pikkit.com)
- **Secondary value prop (bet-tracker page)**: "Track Every Bet You Place" — anchored on the claim that tracking is "the single best indicator of long-term betting skill." (source: https://pikkit.com/bet-tracker)
- **Positioning angle**: Bet tracker + social network hybrid. CLV/closing-line-value is the prestige feature behind the paywall; social ("follow, copy, bet") is the top-of-funnel hook. They straddle "serious analytics tool" and "Strava for bettors."
- **Top 3 messaging themes**
  1. **Automatic, no manual entry** — BookSync as the wedge ("connect your sportsbooks without a subscription or credit card"). Source: /booksync, / home.
  2. **CLV is how you know you're actually winning** — long-term skill framing, Pro-tier gated. Source: /pro.
  3. **Community / copy-trading** — "follow, copy, bet," timeline feed, verified top bettors. Source: / home, /features.
- **Voice/tone**: Friendly-data. Neither stuffy-finance nor bro-degen. Reads like a fitness-tracker app for bettors — emphasis on self-improvement, social proof, transparency. Conspicuously sanitized: "emphasizes responsible gambling."
- **Trust signals on homepage**: 30+ sportsbook logos (DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, ESPN BET, bet365, Fanatics, Hard Rock Bet, BetRivers, etc.), App Store rating (4.9/5), social-feed UI screenshots, "verified" top-bettor accounts in the timeline. No raw user counts or press logos surfaced in this scan.

## Product & Features
**Core capabilities**
- BookSync: automatic, no-manual-entry sync from 30+ US sportsbooks (free).
- Performance dashboard by sport / bet type / team / player.
- CLV (closing-line-value) analysis — Pro only.
- Line shopping across synced books, including SGP line shopping — Pro.
- Odds alerts when markets open or hit a target price — Pro.
- Promo / free-bet / boost-token tracker with expiry notifications — Pro.
- Social timeline: post bets, follow users, see verified top bettors, view community exposure on a market.
- Web access — Pro only (free is mobile-only).

**Notable differentiators they emphasize**
- BookSync is the moat: pulling settled-bet data directly from sportsbook accounts at scale.
- "Build same-game parlays in app and compare prices — exclusively on Pikkit" (per /features search excerpt).
- Social/copy-bet layer that tracker competitors (e.g. Juice Reel, Action Network's tracker) underweight.

**Sportsbook integrations**: 30+ US books via BookSync — DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, ESPN BET, bet365, Fanatics, Hard Rock Bet, BetRivers, theScore Bet, and more. Known gaps: WynnBet sync is reportedly broken; BetMGM-via-Yahoo-login doesn't sync; "a few niche or regional sportsbooks are still missing."

**What they DON'T offer (revealed gaps)**
- **No pre-bet pick / edge engine.** Pikkit tells you whether the bet you already placed beat closing line; it does not tell you which side to bet *before* the market closes. No model-graded edges, no "play this / fade this" recommendation. CLV is rear-view.
- **No narrative or explanatory layer.** Numbers without "why."
- **No "trap" / negative-edge surfacing.** CLV is a post-mortem score, not a pre-bet warning.
- **No AI / LLM angle.** Messaging is all dashboards, syncing, and social.
- **No DFS / props depth signaled** beyond standard book markets.

## Reviews / Sentiment (from web search)
**Common praise**
- Auto-sync is the killer feature — "no manual entry" is repeatedly called out as the reason to switch from spreadsheets.
- Consolidation of multi-book betting history into one dashboard.
- 4.9/5 App Store rating; broadly positive 2025-26 reviews from BetSmart, 8rainstation, SportBot AI, PinnacleOddsDropper.
- Social feed and copy-bet element praised as a differentiator over Juice Reel and Action Network tracker.
- Free tier described as legitimately useful for casual bettors.

**Common complaints**
- **Sync reliability**: 30-minute sync waits, ongoing WynnBet failure, MFA-protected books breaking, "open support tickets for several weeks."
- **App bugs**: stale posts surfacing on relaunch; one App Store reviewer claims "80% of the time" bets aren't tracked or payouts are recorded incorrectly.
- **Pro price**: $39.99/mo is widely flagged as expensive for what is fundamentally a tracker.
- **Free tier ceiling**: CLV — the feature serious bettors actually want — is paywalled, frustrating users who expected the free tier to be enough.
- **Privacy unease**: sharing book credentials to enable BookSync.
- **Referral payout complaints**: users say they didn't get the promised $5–$50 referral credits.

**Representative quotes (paraphrased from review-site summaries; treat as directional, not verbatim)**
- "It solves a clear problem by making bet tracking non-negotiable." — 8rainstation summary.
- "For casual bettors the free version might suffice, but serious bettors may want more robust solutions." — BetSmart summary.

## Competitive Implications for Cray Cray
**Honest framing first**: Pikkit is *not* a direct competitor — it is a **complement that occupies the adjacent jobs-to-be-done.** Pikkit answers "did I bet well?" *after* the bet. Cray Cray answers "should I bet this, and what's the edge?" *before* the bet. They share an audience (multi-book, sharp-curious bettors) but barely overlap in feature surface area. The risk isn't that a user picks Pikkit *instead* of Cray Cray — it's that we let Pikkit own the "serious bettor's tool" mental shelf so completely that we get filed under "fun degen toy" and never get evaluated as a real pre-bet edge product.

**Where they're stronger**
- Brand awareness — they're already "the" bet tracker; 4.9/5 ratings, broad review coverage.
- Distribution moat via BookSync — credential-level integration with 30+ books is an engineering investment we don't have and probably shouldn't try to replicate.
- Social proof / community flywheel — copy-bet timeline gives them organic shareability we lack.
- Free tier that gets them in the door of every multi-book bettor.

**Where we're stronger**
- **Pre-bet edge math.** Per-side signed-pp edge on ML/spread/total is generative work; Pikkit's CLV is descriptive after the fact. We tell you what to do; they grade what you did.
- **Math-derived narrative.** De-Genny's LLM rationale grounded in deterministic edges is something Pikkit's pure-dashboard UX can't produce.
- **The "Trap" label / negative-edge surfacing.** Pikkit's CLV will eventually punish you for betting traps; we flag them *before* you place. This is the most defensible single feature in our stack.
- **Sportsbook-agnostic by design** (we don't sync books, so we don't carry their sync-failure complaint surface).
- **Equal weight to degens** — Pikkit's tone has sanitized away the degen audience. We can credibly serve both sides.

**Copy / positioning we should steal or counter**
- **Steal**: their "single best indicator of long-term betting skill" framing — but flip it from *tracking* to *pre-bet edge discipline*. ("The single best indicator of long-term betting skill is refusing to bet a –12pp trap.")
- **Steal**: the free-no-credit-card top-of-funnel hook.
- **Counter**: Pikkit's "follow, copy, bet" assumes someone else's picks are worth copying. We can counter with "your edge, not someone else's pick" — math you can audit, not influencers you can't.
- **Counter the CLV halo**: many bettors confuse CLV with skill. CLV is lagging; per-side edge is leading. There's a thought-leadership wedge here.

**White space we can claim**
- **"Before the bet" positioning**: pre-bet edge engine paired with post-bet narrative. Pikkit owns post-bet; the pre-bet shelf is wide open.
- **Negative-edge / trap surfacing as a category.** Nobody else publishes this — Pikkit certainly doesn't. Make it a feature, then a category.
- **Auditable AI**: Pikkit doesn't use LLMs at all in user-facing copy. We can own "AI handicapper whose numbers you can check" — math first, narration second.
- **Degen-and-sharp dual voice.** Pikkit ceded the degen voice; we don't have to.
- **Pricing arbitrage**: $39.99/mo is a ceiling reviewers complain about. If we price under it for a *more* generative product, we get a "more value, less money" angle for free.
