# BettorEdge — Competitor Profile

**URL**: https://bettoredge.com
**Generated**: 2026-05-12
**Depth**: quick scan

> ⚠️ **Category note up front:** BettorEdge is **NOT a picks app**. It is a **peer-to-peer (P2P) social betting marketplace / prediction exchange** — users bet against each other, not against a book, at user-set odds with no vig. They are an **adjacent / orthogonal** competitor to Cray Cray for Parlays, not a direct one. The closest overlap is their **BettorEdge Premium** tier, which surfaces analytics (BetMatch, Order Grades, market depth) — but those tools live inside their own marketplace, not as a sportsbook-agnostic handicapping layer like Cray Cray.

## At a Glance
- **Tagline (homepage):** "BettorEdge: Social Betting — Bet, Win, and Connect" (also: "Giving the edge back to bettors")
- **What they actually do:** A P2P sports betting marketplace + prediction exchange. Users post bids/offers at their own chosen odds; the platform matches counterparties. They pitch it as "trade sports like stocks" with no vig and no house edge.
- **Target audience:** Value-conscious bettors who hate the vig, social/community-driven recreational bettors, and exchange-curious users priced out of overseas books (Betfair-style). They also lean into a "fans betting with friends" angle (bar-night promotions, public/private bets).
- **Founded / scale:**
  - Co-founders: **Greg Kajewski** (CEO) and **James Seils** — both ex-PwC consultants, met in 2019.
  - Won SportRadar's first US Innovation Challenge in early 2019.
  - HQ: **Minneapolis, MN**. Public marketing launch Jan 2021.
  - Scale claimed: **15,000+ users**, **$200M+ in submitted orders** (homepage), **$50M+ settled bets** (blog).
  - Awards: SBC Rising Star of the Year; Minne Inno's Best Company to Invest In.
  - Legal in **45+ US states** (because they don't act as the house).

## Product & Features
**Core capabilities (free + Premium):**
- Peer-to-peer marketplace: post bids/offers, market & limit orders, match against other users
- Set your own odds and lines on **moneyline, spread, totals, props**
- Sports covered: NFL, NCAAF, NBA, WNBA, NCAAM, MLB, NHL, UFC, PGA, MLS, LaLiga, Bundesliga, Horse Racing (~13 sports)
- **Flash Markets**: parimutuel live in-game betting (next basket, next score, etc.) — odds shift dynamically with pool action
- **Public & private bets**, head-to-head challenges, group competitions
- **Social feed**: follow users, see their picks, tip creators who share insights
- **Leaderboards** + player profiles with win %, profitability, trends (real-bet-based)
- Pick'ems and Auction Squares (Super Bowl–style party games)
- Sell positions back to the market mid-event (lock in profit/loss like stock trading)

**Premium-only ($19.99/mo):**
- Up to **$750/mo in fee-free orders** (free tier caps at $100/mo before the ~1% fee kicks in on winnings)
- Up to **$5,000 deposits** with priority processing
- **BetMatch** (find good-value orders quickly)
- **Order Grades** (their internal "is this a good bet?" signal)
- Real-time **market depth / liquidity charts**
- **Full Sports Betting API access**
- Free month of **Rithmm** (third-party analytics — $29.99 value)
- Up to **$11.25/mo rebate** on processing fees (effectively cheaper sub)

**Notable differentiators they emphasize:**
- No vig / no house edge (the entire identity)
- User-set odds via limit orders
- Position-trading mechanics (sell mid-event)
- Social/community layer (not just transactional)
- 45-state legality without being a sportsbook (regulatory arbitrage)
- Claim of **40% user profitability** vs. ~2% on traditional books — *unsourced but repeated*

**Sportsbook integrations / partner ecosystem:**
- **They ARE the book replacement** — no DK/FD integration; they're the alternative to those books.
- Partnership/cross-promo with **Rithmm** (analytics).
- Bar/venue partnerships for live group betting (`/social-betting-bar`).
- API offered to third-party builders.

**What they DON'T offer (revealed gaps — opportunity for Cray Cray):**
- **No AI handicapping** — no "should I take this side?" engine; users do their own homework. Order Grades is rudimentary and only signals order quality, not side-edge.
- **Not sportsbook-agnostic** — locked to their own liquidity pool. If a bettor still uses DK/FD/MGM (most do), BettorEdge is dead weight.
- **No deterministic per-side edge math** — no signed edge in pp; no "Trap" warnings on bad lines.
- **No narrative / LLM picks layer** — no equivalent to De-Genny.
- **Liquidity gaps on smaller markets** — well-documented user complaint; props/niche markets often go unmatched.
- **No spread/total/ML edge calculator across books** — they can't compare to a Pinnacle no-vig because they're not on top of the market, they *are* the market.
- **Limited press / mainstream awareness** — Trustpilot has only ~66 reviews despite 15k users; brand is small.

- **Hard to track** which orders are matched day-to-day (UX gap)
- Smaller bettor pool than mainstream books → less depth

## Competitive Implications for Cray Cray

### Where BettorEdge is stronger than us
- **They solve a real economic pain (the vig)** — degens viscerally understand "no juice" in a way they don't yet understand "+8.2pp edge." Their hook converts.
- **Built-in monetary mechanism**: $10 sign-up bonus, referral payouts, tipping creators. We don't have a money-on-the-line hook.
- **Real bet history per user** (leaderboards based on actual matched bets) — credible social proof we can't match without a settlement layer linked to real accounts.
- **Regulatory positioning**: legal in 45 states; we depend on the sportsbooks our users already use.
- **Bigger content/SEO surface area** — they publish heavily on prediction markets, vig calculators, no-vig calculators (`/novig-calculator`, `/hold-calculator`, `/fee-calculator`). Strong free-tool SEO play.

### Where Cray Cray is stronger
- **We're sportsbook-agnostic.** Their users still need a "should I take this?" brain. We're that brain — for any book.
- **Deterministic per-side edge math** in pp is more rigorous than their "Order Grades." We show *which side* and *how much*; they grade *order quality* on their internal marketplace.
- **The "Trap" label / negative edges** — they have nothing equivalent. BettorEdge users still have to figure out *what to bet*; they only solve *where to bet it*. Negative edges are a wedge BettorEdge literally cannot copy without contradicting their "free market" thesis.
- **AI narrative layer (De-Genny)** — they have no LLM picks-rationale equivalent. "Why this side" beats raw order grades for both degens and sharp-curious.
- **No liquidity dependency** — we don't need a counterparty; our edge is independent of pool depth.
- **Math-derived narrative** — our copy can show numbers AND speak human. Their voice is fintech-formal; ours is bolder.

### Copy / positioning we should steal or counter
- **Steal (frame, not words):** Their "Give the edge back to bettors" frame is excellent — it positions the user as the victim of a rigged system and the product as the fix. Cray Cray should adopt an equivalent frame: *"The book hides which side has the edge. We show it."* Same victim → savior pattern, different villain.
- **Steal (free tools as SEO):** Their `/novig-calculator`, `/hold-calculator`, `/fee-calculator`, `/betting-calculator` pages are excellent SEO bait + brand authority builders. We should ship a **Cray Cray Edge Calculator** (paste any line, get the pp edge) as a free public tool — converts AND ranks.
- **Counter:** They claim "40% user profitability vs. 2% on traditional books" without sourcing. We can win on credibility by showing **transparent grading methodology** and a public hit-rate page (we already have a ResultsPage — this is a known strength to amplify).
- **Counter their CTA:** "Start Winning More Today" is generic. Our equivalent should be more specific: *"See tonight's edges in 60 seconds"* or *"Find tonight's traps."*
- **Counter their voice:** They sound like a fintech startup. Lean into the De-Genny voice on the landing page — it's a differentiator their tone literally cannot copy without breaking brand.

### White space we can claim
1. **"The brain for any sportsbook"** — explicitly position as the agnostic handicapping layer. BettorEdge can never be agnostic; they *are* a book. Make this our headline wedge.
2. **"We show negative edges"** — own the trap/fade angle. BettorEdge will never publish "this side is a trap" because their entire business model is matchmaking *all* orders. We can be the only player saying "don't bet this one."
3. **The math-grounded narrative** — frame Cray Cray as *"pp-grade math + a degen who can explain it."* BettorEdge gives you tools; we give you a verdict with a receipt.
4. **Degen-coded voice** — they're fintech-clean. There's a wide-open lane for a confident, slangy, *funny* picks brand that still backs every claim with deterministic math. De-Genny is built exactly for this.
5. **Sharp-curious bridge** — degens get the swagger, sharp-curious get the math. BettorEdge serves neither cohort cleanly: too technical for casuals, too liquidity-limited for sharps.
6. **Free Edge Calculator tool** — directly counter their `/novig-calculator` SEO play with our own version that shows signed per-side edge across any book's line.
